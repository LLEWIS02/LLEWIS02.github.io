I'm Lauren Lewis.

Media Production Student & Content Creator

Bio

Lauren Lewis is a senior majoring in Audiovisual Media Production at Howard Community College, where she pours her passion for creating into every project, blending film, editing, and design. What started as a hobby in high school capturing moments and experimenting with creative software quickly turned into a career path centered around crafting visual stories. Whether she’s writing and illustrating a 50-page graphic novel, creating video shorts, or designing promotional content, Lauren’s work is all about pushing creative boundaries and bringing ideas to life with authenticity and a bit (a lot) of eccentricity. For her, every project is a chance to create something meaningful: stories that don't follow the leader, inform, and inspire. Outside of class, Lauren is deeply involved in her HCC community. She works at the wellness center, creating content that supports mental and physical well-being, making sure students have the tools they need to thrive. Working at the campus food pantry has also taught her the importance of accessibility, helping make the campus more inclusive. She’s also honing her skills in media at The Howard Courier Newspaper, where she’s gaining experience in communication, marketing, and audience engagement, all while learning how to blend creativity with business strategy. Looking ahead, Lauren wants to create media that is at the center of change with content that encourages people to embrace their individuality and continue learning and creating. She hopes to use her skills to support businesses, non-profits, and causes that align with her values, uplifting those that deserve to be seen and heard. When she’s not at school or working, Lauren enjoys making her own clothes, drawing, filming, longboarding, and always learning something new.
